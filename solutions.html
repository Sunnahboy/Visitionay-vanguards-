Alternative Packaging options:

○ Biodegradable Plastics: Biodegradable polymers that break down naturally over time, reducing environmental impact.
○ Compostable Packaging: Can be composted along with organic waste, offering sustainable end-of-life solution.
○ Plant-Based Plastics: Uses renewable resources such as corn, sugarcane, or cellulose to produce plastics derived from plants rather than fossil fuels.
○ Mushroom Packaging: Mycelium, root structure of mushrooms, used to create packaging material that are both biodegradable and composable.
○ Edible Packaging: Safe for consumption or can be dissolved in water, eliminates need for disposal and reducing waste.
○ Paper-Based Packaging: Uses recycled or sustainably sourced paper materials, biodegradable and recyclable.
○ Reusable Packaging: Durable and reusable packaging containers or systems. Can be returned, refilled, or repurposed multiple times. Reduces need for single-use packaging.
○ Seaweed Packaging: Is biodegradable, renewable, and potentially edible.
○ Textile-Based Packaging: Flexible and lightweight packaging solutions that are reusable and recyclable.
○ Nanotechnology-Based Packaging: Enhanced properties such as barrier strength, durability, and biodegradability.
